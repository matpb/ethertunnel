For most people that's all you need. This document is for the minority who need
to step outside the AGPL — it explains exactly what a commercial license buys,
what it doesn't require, and how to get one.

## TL;DR — do you even need this?

**Probably not.** A commercial license is *only* relevant if the AGPL's
source-sharing obligation is a problem for what you're building. If you're just
running EtherTunnel for yourself or your company, you can stop reading — keep
using it under the AGPL, for free, forever.

## What the AGPL already lets you do, for free

No commercial license is required to:

- **Self-host the unmodified relay** — run it for yourself, your team, or your
  whole company, internally or public-facing, at any scale. Unmodified
  self-hosting triggers no copyleft obligation to anyone.
- **Modify it for your own internal use** and never distribute or expose those
  changes as a service to outside users.
- **Fork it, study it, redistribute it** under the AGPL, with source.

If that covers you, you're done. The AGPL costs nothing and asks for nothing
back in these cases.

## What the AGPL requires (and what the commercial license waives)

The AGPL's one real obligation is **network copyleft** (its section 13): if you
run a **modified** version of the relay as a service that other people interact
with over a network, you must offer those users the complete corresponding
source of your modified version under the AGPL. The same reciprocity applies if
you distribute a modified relay.

A **commercial license waives exactly that**. It grants you the right to:

- **Run a modified EtherTunnel relay as a service** without publishing your
  modifications.
- **Embed EtherTunnel inside a closed-source product** and distribute it without
  AGPL source-sharing or same-license obligations on your surrounding code.
- Keep your proprietary changes, integrations, and competitive edge private.

In short: the commercial license is the AGPL's network-copyleft and
same-license terms lifted, by private agreement, for you.

## What it does *not* do

- It does not change the open-source project — EtherTunnel stays AGPL for
  everyone else.
- It does not include hosting, SLAs, or support by default. Those can be added,
  but the base license is a license, not a support contract — say so if you need
  them and we'll scope it in.
- It does not transfer copyright. You license usage rights; authorship stays
  with the project.
